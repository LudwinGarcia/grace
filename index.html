Main Problem

Guatemala has the fifth largest percentage of chronically malnourished people in the world, with over 60% of the population being affected in the worst regions.

The worst effects of chronic malnutrition are parasitical infection, diarrhea and hematomas - which if not treated correctly can cause death. These physical ailments have a damaging effect on the intellectual development of children.

Our Mission

We reach out to rural communities by spreading awareness of the benefits of the Moringa Tree, educating those in need about its effective usage to quell the spread of Chronic Malnutrition across rural Guatemala. Helping people to help themselves by providing the tools needed to lead happy and healthy lives.

Main Problem?

Guatemala is the fifth country in the whole world with chronic malnutrition. Some of the affected departments are Zacapa with 59.5%, Chiquimula with 63.16% and Jalapa with 54.66% of chronic malnutrition. The strongest effects of malnutrition are: acute respiratory illnesses, diarrhea, hair loss, parasite infection, lack of defenses, deficit of intellectual development, lower productivity, low academic retention, infertility, hematomas, and if not treated correctly it can even cause death.
