Main Problem?

Guatemala is the fifth country in the whole world with chronic malnutrition. Some of the affected departments are Zacapa with 59.5%, Chiquimula with 63.16% and Jalapa with 54.66% of chronic malnutrition. The strongest effects of malnutrition are: acute respiratory illnesses, diarrhea, hair loss, parasite infection, lack of defenses, deficit of intellectual development, lower productivity, low academic retention, infertility, hematomas, and if not treated correctly it can even cause death.
